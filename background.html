Context and Policy Landscape

Medicare Part D and Formulary Restrictiveness

Medicare Part D, enacted as part of the Medicare Modernization Act of 2003, provides voluntary outpatient prescription drug coverage to Medicare beneficiaries through private insurance plans. These plans maintain formularies — structured lists of covered drugs — with varying levels of coverage determined by drug tier, prior authorization (PA) requirements, step therapy protocols, and quantity limits.

Formulary restrictiveness directly shapes medication access for the approximately 50 million beneficiaries enrolled in Part D plans. Prior authorization — a process requiring prescribers to obtain insurer approval before coverage is granted — represents the most clinically disruptive form of formulary restriction, associated with treatment delays, increased administrative burden, and care discontinuation.

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists: Clinical and Policy Significance

GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs), including semaglutide (Ozempic®, Wegovy®), dulaglutide (Trulicity®), and liraglutide (Victoza®), represent a transformative advance in type 2 diabetes management. These agents demonstrate superior glycemic control alongside cardiovascular and renal protective effects in landmark clinical trials, leading to expanded clinical guidelines recommending their use in patients with established cardiovascular disease, heart failure, or chronic kidney disease.

The same pharmacologic mechanisms that confer metabolic benefit — GLP-1 pathway activation producing sustained satiety and reduced caloric intake — have driven dramatic off-label adoption for weight management. Medicare Part D does not cover obesity pharmacotherapy, creating an administrative challenge for plans seeking to limit coverage to diabetes-indicated use.

Policy Timeline

2020
Baseline Period

GLP-1 PA requirements minimal (~7%). Medicare Senior Savings Model launches, capping insulin out-of-pocket costs for participating plans.

2022
Inflation Reduction Act

IRA transforms Part D benefit design: $2,000 catastrophic cap, $35 insulin cap, manufacturer rebate provisions. Enhanced subsidies expand low-income enrollment.

2023
Semaglutide Demand Surge

Ozempic and Wegovy achieve cultural saturation. Off-label prescription volume for weight loss accelerates. Plans begin anticipating formulary cost exposure.

2024
PA Surge · IRA Benefit Redesign

GLP-1 PA rates surge to ~78% nationally. IRA benefit redesign takes full effect — restructured cost-sharing tiers incentivize plan-level formulary tightening.

2025
Near-Universal PA Requirement

GLP-1 PA rates reach ~100% across Medicare Part D plans. Formulary restrictiveness for GLP-1s now nearly universal, despite continued strong cardiovascular evidence base.
